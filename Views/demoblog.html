Gondola was not a mistake.

Gondola is the perfect metaphor, in many ways, to our own situation.
He appears in various places, all over the world, all throughout time, with no effort at all. Incredible right? Just like us, really; with the click of a button, we can view any piece of art, any historical event, in the blink of an eye. With hardly a lifting a finger, we are, like Gondola, able to be anywhere, see anything. That is life on the internet, the joy of the modern world.
But he is powerless to act. He has no arms, only legs. He is only an silent observer; all he can do is go from one thing to the next. He cannot interact with the things he sees, he is powerless to prevent tragedies that have long since happened even though he may revisit them time and time again. Having been exposed to suffering and terror and gloom unlike any of our ancestors could have been outside a dictator's meat grinder every night on the evening news (Mali hotel killings, Paris terror attacks, a thousand atrocities reported every day before the late night shows come on), we are like Gondola.
We observe; we comment. We carry on. We cannot do much else.
And like him, we find there is no point in being sad. An unintrusive, warmly welcomed melancholy descends upon us. To worry about things we cannot change would drive us mad - but still we are driven to see them, to wonder. To silently observe. And then move on. There is so much to see, after all, even if you cannot touch.
